About

I learned data the way most analysts never do: by carrying a quota first.

For five years I sold windows, doors, cabinets, and OEM products to US contractors and businesses, remotely from Lagos. Pipelines, quotes, follow-up cadences, CRM hygiene, the whole revenue engine. Then I trained the full analytics stack to explain the numbers I used to live inside.

The short version

Most analysts learn the tools first and the business later. I did it in the other order. Working US B2B sales in building materials and manufacturing meant every number had a person and a decision behind it: a contractor deciding whether to order, a manager deciding which lead to chase. When I analyze retention, discounting, or operational load now, I am asking the operator's question: what do we do differently on Monday?

I completed a structured analytics program covering Excel, Power Query, Power Pivot, Power BI, Tableau, and SQL, and built five fully packaged case studies to prove it, each with documented code, a written case study, an executive summary, and a deck. I hold myself to one rule across all of them: no number without a named source, and no claim the data cannot support. The green chips across this site are that rule made visible.
